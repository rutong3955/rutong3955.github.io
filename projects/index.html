research portfolio

My research experience spans experimental evaluations, policy analysis, and field implementation across education and health sectors in developing countries, with a focus on China.

Field Research Projects

Throughout these projects, my responsibilities typically included: drafting funding proposals, designing study protocols and survey questionnaires, training enumerators and field staff, organizing and supervising data collection, conducting data cleaning and analysis, and writing research articles， grant reports, and policy briefs.

Community-based Support for Early Learning and Responsive Care for Children Aged 0-6 Years in China
November 2023–Present
Evaluating the impacts of a community-based ECD project on children’s cognitive and non-cognitive development outcomes in 40 counties in China.

Evaluating the Impact of Special-designed Music Class on Rural Minority Children’s Non-cognitive Abilities
October 2023–Present
A quasi-experimental study examining the effects of music class intervention on students’ non-cognitive skills in rural Southwest China.

Improving the Information Literacy of Students from Primary and Senior Schools in Northwestern China
May 2023–Present
Exploring effective methods of developing students’ information literacy and identifying a practical implementation toolkit in Northwest China.

Identifying the Impacts and Mechanisms of a Computer-Assisted Learning (CAL) Intervention on Students’ Educational Outcomes
July 2019–Present
A randomized controlled trial evaluating the impacts of CAL intervention on students’ academic performance, non-cognitive skills, and long-term returns in 202 rural schools in Northwest China.

Building a Foundation for Human Capital Development in the Field of Education in Western China
September 2016–December 2019
Established an Experimental Economics in Education (EEE) discipline and research platform in the field of education based in Western China.

Evaluating the Impact of Health Information Intervention on Students’ Health and Educational Outcomes in Rural Tibetan Areas
September 2016–December 2016
Examining the effects of health information intervention on students’ health and academic performance in 58 rural schools in Tibetan areas of China.

Using Standardized Patients Method to Measure Healthcare Quality in Rural China
February 2015–December 2015
Collecting three waves of panel data from 279 village clinic physicians and 654 township health center physicians across three provinces to assess healthcare quality.

Pay-for-performance: Evaluating the Impact of Various Teacher Incentive Plans on Students’ Academic Performance
May 2014–June 2015
Assessing which teacher incentive schemes are most effective in improving the academic performance of rural students.

Seeing is Learning: Evaluating the Impact of Vision Care on Students’ Academic Performance
May 2014–June 2015
A randomized controlled trial assessing the educational impact of providing eyeglasses to near-sighted students in 300 rural schools in Northwest China.

Nourishing the Future: Targeting Infants and Their Caregivers to Reduce Undernutrition
August 2014–May 2015
A randomized controlled trial examining the effects of early intervention on infants’ health and development outcomes in 1,802 rural households in Northwest China.

Using Conditional Cash Transfer (CCT) to Improve Maternal and Child Health (MCH) Services in Rural China
August 2014–December 2014
Understanding and strengthening the use of MCH services through CCT intervention in 27 minority villages in Northwest China.
